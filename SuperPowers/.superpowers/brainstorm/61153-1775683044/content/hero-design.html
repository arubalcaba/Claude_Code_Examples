Section 2: Hero Section Design

The 3D centerpiece — a neural mesh with dithered surfaces responding to mouse movement

3D Scene Composition

Neural Lattice Hero

A 3D lattice of interconnected nodes floating in space. Nodes glow cyan, edges pulse with data flow. Ordered dither shader applied as surface texture on triangulated faces between nodes. The whole structure slowly rotates and responds to mouse position with subtle parallax.

Hero Technical Details

1

Geometry

IcosahedronGeometry with detail level 3-4, giving ~320 triangular faces. Vertices displaced with simplex noise for organic irregularity. Wireframe edges rendered as a separate pass with LineBasicMaterial for the lattice look.

2

Dither Shader (GLSL)

Fragment shader applies an 8x8 Bayer matrix dither pattern to the surface lighting. Instead of smooth gradients, light falloff becomes a pattern of lit/unlit pixels. The dither threshold animates slowly over time, creating a "breathing" effect.

3

Mouse Interaction

Mouse position mapped to gentle rotation (±15° max). Smooth lerp interpolation so it feels fluid, not jittery. On mobile, slow auto-rotation replaces mouse tracking.

4

Post-Processing

Subtle bloom pass on the node points and edge highlights. A vignette darkens the edges. No motion blur — keep it crisp to let the dither pattern read clearly.

Text Overlay on Hero

T

Company Name + Tagline + CTA

Centered HTML overlay (not part of the 3D scene). Company name in bold monospace or geometric sans-serif. Tagline in smaller cyan text. "Get Started" button with cyan border, fills on hover. Text has a slight text-shadow glow for readability over the mesh.
